About
I study how India is governed — by tracing its districts, schools, and the people who shaped them.

My research sits at the intersection of computational science and public policy. Starting from a PhD in Physics, I now study India's administrative evolution, education governance, and public health — bringing quantitative rigour to historical and policy questions. I lead IndiaStateStory, tracing India's district boundaries from 1872 to the present.

Research

Areas of Inquiry

My work spans three interconnected fields: the political and administrative history of India, education policy and governance, and computational epidemiology. Each strand uses quantitative and qualitative methods to address questions of governance, equity, and public welfare.

I
Education Policy & Governance
Studying school systems, Right to Education (RTE) implementation, and language policies in Indian education.
II
India's Administrative Evolution
Characterizing district proliferation and boundary changes from the colonial era to contemporary India.
III
Federalism & Decentralization
Analyzing political transitions, internal migration, and the evolution of urban and local governance.
IV
Computational Epidemiology
Modeling the spread of vector-borne diseases like Dengue and analyzing public health responses.
V
Social Impact & Welfare
Evaluating the impact of COVID-19 on unorganized workers and migrant distress in urban India.
VI
Network Science
Exploring co-authorship networks and citation dynamics within the scientific community in India.

Featured Project

India State & District Evolution

Tracing the political and administrative history of India since 1872.

Curated by Dr. Shivakumar Jolad and research associate Mehr Kalra, this project maps the evolution of administrative boundaries, linking them with India's complex political history from the Colonial period to the present.
